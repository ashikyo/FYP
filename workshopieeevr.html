Locomotion Vault

Unifying Research on VR Locomotion

Proposed Workshop for IEEE VR 2021, March 27, Lisbon

No silver bullet exists for moving about in a Virtual Reality (VR) environment. Hundreds of techniques have been invented for VR locomotion in the last decade each with their unique and sometimes idiosyncratic characteristics. Newly proposed locomotion techniques (LTs) are usually compared to one or a few other LTs at best. Researchers have proposed several taxonomies and criteria for describing and evaluating LTs, (e.g. in terms of the hardware employed, their granularity, accessibility). Yet, no agreement exists on which evaluation criteria or taxonomies are the most important when searching for an LT or designing a new one.

Our goal is to unify research in the field by creating shared community-sourced evaluation criteria and datasets for locomotion in VR. To this end, we have created LocomotionVault (https://locomotionvault.github.io/), an interactive visual database of 109 VR LTs from academia and industry. Each LT is described according to X attributes that we collated from existing VR taxonomies.

In this workshop, we aim to bring VR locomotion researchers together to discuss existing taxonomies and evaluation criteria for VR LTs, challenges in design and evaluation of LTs in such a rapidly-growing space, and potential community-driven tools and initiatives that can move the field forward. We aim to tackle these important topics with short lightning talks of the accepted abstracts followed by lively discussions with the attendees.

Workshop topics

  • VR locomotion techniques
  • Locomotion taxonomies
  • Evaluation criteria and methods for VR locomotion
